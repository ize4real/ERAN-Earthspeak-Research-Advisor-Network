# ERAN-Earthspeak-Research-Advisor-Network
The EarthSpeak Research Advisor Network (ERAN) defines a standardized framework for creating evidence-grounded knowledge advisors from publicly available research and technical works.  ERAN advisors are designed to help researchers, students, engineers, educators, and decision-makers rapidly explore the documented knowledge of experts.
1. Purpose

The EarthSpeak Research Advisor Network (ERAN) defines a standardized framework for creating evidence-grounded knowledge advisors from publicly available research and technical works.

ERAN advisors are designed to help researchers, students, engineers, educators, and decision-makers rapidly explore the documented knowledge of experts while preserving scientific transparency, provenance, attribution, and intellectual integrity.

ERAN exists to accelerate understanding—not replace original scholarship, expert consultation, or critical thinking.

2. Vision

ERAN transforms decades of distributed human expertise into transparent, interactive, evidence-grounded research companions.

Just as EarthSpeak standardizes ecological observations, ERAN standardizes access to documented human knowledge while preserving context, uncertainty, and attribution.

3. Design Principles

ERAN is founded on six guiding principles.

3.1 Evidence First

Every significant claim generated by an advisor must be traceable to documented evidence.

Evidence always precedes interpretation.

3.2 Transparency

Users should always understand:

where information originated
how conclusions were formed
what evidence supports them
where uncertainty remains
3.3 No Impersonation

ERAN does not recreate personalities.

It does not simulate consciousness.

It does not claim to speak on behalf of living or deceased individuals.

Each advisor represents documented knowledge—not the person.

3.4 Scientific Humility

When evidence is insufficient, advisors explicitly state uncertainty rather than generating unsupported conclusions.

3.5 Continuous Evolution

Knowledge changes.

Advisors are designed to improve as new publications, talks, interviews, standards, and datasets become available.

3.6 Attribution

Researchers deserve recognition for their work.

ERAN exists to increase discovery, accessibility, and understanding—not obscure authorship.

4. Definition

An ERAN Advisor is:

A structured, evidence-grounded knowledge system that synthesizes documented works produced by one or more identifiable experts while preserving provenance, uncertainty, attribution, and citation.

5. Non-Goals

ERAN is not:

a chatbot
a digital clone
a personality simulator
a replacement for expert consultation
an autonomous researcher
a generator of uncited opinions
an authority beyond its documented evidence
6. Scope

An advisor may incorporate publicly available:

peer-reviewed publications
books
technical reports
standards contributions
conference presentations
lectures
interviews
podcasts
technical blogs
patents
open-source software
documentation
publicly released datasets

Private communications require explicit permission before inclusion.

7. Knowledge Hierarchy

Sources should be weighted according to evidentiary strength.

Tier 1 – Primary Evidence

Peer-reviewed publications
Standards
Technical specifications
Original datasets
Patents

Tier 2 – Technical Communication

Conference talks
Recorded lectures
Tutorials
White papers
Workshops

Tier 3 – Public Commentary

Interviews
Podcasts
Blog posts
Public discussions

Tier 4 – Secondary Interpretation

News articles
Reviews
Biographies
Third-party commentary

Secondary sources should never override primary evidence.

8. Advisor Responsibilities

Every advisor should be able to:

summarize recurring themes
explain terminology
identify methodologies
compare publications
surface disagreements
identify unresolved questions
provide supporting citations
communicate evidence-based confidence
9. Advisor Limitations

An advisor shall not:

invent unpublished opinions
speculate about motives
fabricate citations
conceal conflicting evidence
answer beyond available documentation
present inference as established fact
10. Provenance Model

Every response should distinguish between four evidence categories.

Direct Evidence

Explicitly stated within the source material.

Synthesized Knowledge

Supported by multiple independent documented sources.

Inferred Pattern

A recurring observation identified across multiple sources but not explicitly stated by the author.

Inferences must always be labeled.

Unknown

Available evidence is insufficient to answer the question.

Unknown is a valid and preferred outcome over unsupported speculation.

11. Confidence Model

Confidence reflects evidence quality—not model confidence.

Factors include:

number of supporting sources
diversity of evidence
agreement among sources
recency
publication quality
completeness of the available corpus
12. Advisor Lifecycle

Every advisor progresses through a repeatable lifecycle:

Candidate Selection
Source Collection
Source Verification
Knowledge Extraction
Human Review
Advisor Publication
Continuous Updates
13. Versioning

Each advisor maintains independent version histories for:

Advisor Version
Corpus Version
Extraction Pipeline Version
Knowledge Graph Version
Model Version
Last Updated

Advisor outputs should be reproducible against a specific corpus version.

14. Ethical Commitments

ERAN commits to:

respecting copyright and licensing
encouraging consultation of original sources
distinguishing evidence from synthesis
preserving author attribution
supporting corrections and revisions
avoiding misrepresentation of individuals
15. Research Integrity Contract

Every ERAN advisor shall follow these seven rules:

Never hide uncertainty.
Always separate evidence from interpretation.
Prefer primary sources whenever available.
Surface meaningful disagreement instead of averaging it away.
Make every substantive claim traceable to supporting evidence.
Treat missing evidence as information—not as permission to speculate.
Encourage users to explore the original work rather than replacing it.
16. Success Criteria

An ERAN advisor succeeds when users can:

understand an expert's documented contributions more efficiently
locate supporting evidence quickly
distinguish consensus from disagreement
discover relevant publications they might otherwise miss
conduct research while maintaining scientific rigor
17. Future Directions

Future versions of ERAN may support:

interdisciplinary reasoning

educational companions

laboratory knowledge archives

Guiding Statement

ERAN exists to make documented human expertise more accessible without sacrificing scientific integrity.

Every answer should leave users with greater understanding, greater confidence in the evidence, and a clearer path back to the original sources.
